# Kipping ring muscle-up block

> A 6-week kipping ring-MU progression. The throughline is **frequency over
> load** (SKILL.md §5): chase reps and positions, don't grind. False-grip
> strength and a clean transition turnover are the two limiters this block
> attacks. Drills are skill work — no calculator loads here.

## Week 1
Cue: Own the false grip first. Slow, strict, no swing yet.
- False-grip ring rows — 4x6
- False-grip hang — 4x20s
- Eccentric lower from support — 4x3 (slow)
- Hollow/arch ring kip swing — 4x5

## Week 2
Cue: Build the swing rhythm; keep the false grip glued through the pull.
- False-grip ring rows — 4x6
- Ring kip swing (hips to rings) — 5x4
- Transition drill on low rings (feet assist) — 5x3
- Support hold + dips — 3x5

## Week 3
Cue: This week's focus — false-grip strength + the transition turnover. Fast
elbows around the rings, chase frequency not load.
- False-grip ring pull-ups — 4x4
- Low-ring transitions (feet assist, minimal) — 5x3
- Kip swing to high pull (sternum to rings) — 5x4
- Eccentric lower from support — 4x3

## Week 4
Cue: Link the kip to the turnover. Banded full reps to feel the whole path.
- Banded ring MU (full path) — 5x3
- Kip swing to high pull — 5x4
- Low-ring transitions (no feet) — 4x3
- False-grip ring rows — 3x6

## Week 5
Cue: Reduce assistance. One crisp rep beats three sloppy ones.
- Ring MU (light band or feet) — 5x2
- Strict-ish transition negatives — 4x3
- Kip swing to high pull — 4x4
- Support dips — 3x5

## Week 6
Cue: Test/express week — attempt unassisted singles when fresh, then back off.
- Ring MU attempts (unassisted singles) — practice EMOM x10
- Banded ring MU — 3x3 (volume back-off)
- False-grip hang — 3x20s
