# My usual training week

This is the athlete's general weekly availability — **when** classes are on and
which option is preferred each day. It is the day spine you map the week's class
programming onto. It does **not** encode the day's stimulus or any loads (those
come from the pasted programming and the calculator).

> **When my week differs from this, I'll tell you in chat** (e.g. "Thursday I can
> only do the comp class", "this week is PM-only", "rest Wednesday", "Saturday is
> weightlifting-only"). Take what I say as an override for that week and otherwise
> assume the usual week below.

## Default week

| Day | Usual sessions | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | 5:30pm CrossFit + 6:30pm Weightlifting | On a hard week, swap to the **5:30am CrossFit + 6:30pm WL** double. |
| **Tue** | 5:30am CrossFit | Move to 5:30pm if training PM that day. |
| **Wed** | 5:30am Weightlifting + 5:30pm CrossFit | Preferred. (PM-only alt: 5:30pm CrossFit + 6:30pm WL.) |
| **Thu** | 5:30pm Gymnastics + 6:30pm Comp class | Sometimes just the 6:30pm comp class, or 4:30pm gymnastics only. |
| **Fri** | Rest | Usually free for **open gym during the day** (works from home) — good slot for make-up or low-CNS work. |
| **Sat** | 7am CrossFit + 8am Weightlifting | Two variants: **8am WL only** when weightlifting is the priority (drop the 7am CF, train fresh); **8am WL + 9:30am CrossFit** only when a double is needed and WL is heavy. |
| **Sun** | Rest | Optional **open gym before noon** for skill, rehab, or upper-body strength. |

## How to use it

- Treat the **default sessions** as what's on unless I say otherwise for the week.
- Where a day has multiple class slots (e.g. Wed AM WL + PM CF), sequence
  **strength before conditioning** per the policy, and place protected strength on
  the **freshest** day available.
- Fri/Sun open gym are flex slots: use them for make-up skill, rehab, or a missed
  protected lift — never to overwrite a genuine rest day unless I ask.
- If I describe a week that doesn't fit this at all (travel, deload, an event),
  just ask me what's available and build from that.
