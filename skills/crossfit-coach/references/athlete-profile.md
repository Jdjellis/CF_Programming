# Athlete profile

The athlete: an intermediate CrossFitter (35+) training for RX competition, with a
goal of competing at the **2026 Torian Pro (35+)** within ~3 years. The priority
right now is building strength and fitness *foundations* and attacking specific
**limiters**, not peaking for the Open or a competition. Trains at CrossFit
Claremont and follows class programming, complemented by focused personal work.

This file is the *why* behind the policy. When you tier, deconflict, or pick
accessory work, reason from this diagnostic — it explains every rule in
`policy.md`.

## Diagnostic read (the limiters)

- **Front squat is the keystone limiter.** FS (~135) is only ~82% of back squat
  (~165), under the 85–90% norm, and the clean sits at ~92% of FS (far too high).
  The clean goal needs an FS near 160. Highest-leverage strength target.
- **Cleans are recovery-limited, not catch-height.** The stand-up out of the front
  rack is the bottleneck (power clean ~98% of full clean). Fix with heavy + paused
  front squats, block cleans, halting cleans. **Do not program "drop under" /
  fast-elbow catch drills** — they don't address the limiter.
- **Snatch is technique-limited (pull finish), not leg-limited.** Snatch is ~53% of
  back squat (norm 60–65%); the fault is finishing the 2nd/3rd pull before diving
  under. Fix with pull-finish work, not overhead-stability work.
- **Overhead pressing is the genuine weak link.** Strict press ~70 vs 80 goal;
  strict HSPU is a target. Box programming under-supplies strict pressing volume —
  this is a protected *independent* lift.
- **Huge posterior pull, lagging quads/front rack.** Back squat is only ~69% of
  deadlift; the deadlift goal is already exceeded. **Deprioritise pulling/deadlift
  volume**; bias quad- and front-rack-dominant work.
